\abstract{
    \noindent
    This thesis addresses a variant of the classical Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) in which the traveler must complete a journey while managing limited resources—such as food, water, or stamina—and navigating through a partially observable environment fraught with uncertainty. We introduce a probabilistic path planning framework that extends traditional TSP formulations to account for stochastic hazards (e.g., enemy encounters or blocked paths) and resource depletion. The proposed model balances route optimality with survival likelihood, integrating constraint-aware heuristics and risk estimation methods. 

    \noindent
    To evaluate our approach, we conducted extensive simulations based on terrain and travel data inspired by real-life conditions across Middle-earth, modeling key challenges such as unpredictable orc ambushes and the scarcity of safe resting points. These experiments demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in planning resilient, efficient routes under adverse conditions. This work lays a foundation for future exploration in robust autonomous navigation and constrained optimization under uncertainty.
}
